# Case Study – Nykaa IPO Analysis

## Project Overview

This project analyzes Nykaa's financial performance during the five years following its IPO (FY2022–FY2026). The objective was to evaluate whether the company's financial performance ultimately justified the premium valuation investors paid at the time of listing.

The analysis was performed using publicly available annual reports and focuses on financial statement analysis, ratio analysis, trend analysis, and executive reporting.

---

## Business Question

**Did Nykaa's financial performance justify the premium valuation investors paid during its IPO?**

---

## Project Objectives

- Analyze revenue growth after the IPO.
- Evaluate profitability trends.
- Assess cash flow performance.
- Review changes in the financial position.
- Present findings through an executive dashboard.
- Form an evidence-based investment conclusion.

## Key Findings

- Revenue increased from **₹3,774 Cr** to **₹10,022 Cr**, delivering a **21.6% CAGR**.
- Operating Margin improved from **4.35%** to **7.50%**.
- Operating and Free Cash Flow turned positive during the later years.
- Assets expanded significantly while borrowings also increased.
- Overall, Nykaa strengthened its financial position after listing, although profitability still has room for improvement.

---

## Final Conclusion

Based on this analysis, I concluded that **Nykaa has partially justified its premium IPO valuation**. The company demonstrated strong revenue growth, improving profitability, and healthier cash flows after listing. However, continued improvement in profit margins and efficient management of borrowings will be important for creating greater long-term shareholder value.
